# HIPAA Compliance for Clinics in Texas

**Texas clinics must comply with federal HIPAA plus state-specific regulations. Here's what you need to know.**

HIPAA is federal law, but Texas has additional requirements. You need to comply with both federal HIPAA and Texas-specific regulations including the Texas Medical Records Privacy Act.

## Federal HIPAA Requirements

**All Texas clinics must comply with:**
- HIPAA Privacy Rule
- HIPAA Security Rule
- HIPAA Breach Notification Rule
- All 9 required policies
- Risk assessment
- Staff training
- Business Associate Agreements (BAAs)

**See our [Complete HIPAA Compliance Guide](/blog/complete-hipaa-compliance-guide) for federal requirements.**

## Texas-Specific Requirements

### 1. Texas Medical Records Privacy Act

**What it covers:**
- Medical records privacy
- Patient consent requirements
- Breach notification
- Patient access rights

**Key differences from HIPAA:**
- Additional patient rights
- Stricter consent requirements for some disclosures
- State-specific breach notification requirements

### 2. Texas Breach Notification

**Requirements:**
- Notify patients within 60 days (same as HIPAA)
- Notify Texas Attorney General for breaches affecting 250+ residents
- Additional notification requirements

**HIPAA requirement:** 60 days
**Texas requirement:** 60 days (same as HIPAA)

**You must comply with both requirements.**
