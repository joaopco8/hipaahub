# HIPAA Compliance for Clinics in New Jersey

**New Jersey clinics must comply with federal HIPAA plus state-specific regulations. Here's what you need to know.**

HIPAA is federal law, but New Jersey has additional requirements. You need to comply with both federal HIPAA and New Jersey-specific regulations.

## Federal HIPAA Requirements

**All New Jersey clinics must comply with:**
- HIPAA Privacy Rule
- HIPAA Security Rule
- HIPAA Breach Notification Rule
- All 9 required policies
- Risk assessment
- Staff training
- Business Associate Agreements (BAAs)

**See our [Complete HIPAA Compliance Guide](/blog/complete-hipaa-compliance-guide) for federal requirements.**

## New Jersey-Specific Requirements

### 1. State Breach Notification

**Requirements:**
- Notify patients within 60 days (same as HIPAA)
- Notify New Jersey Attorney General for breaches affecting 500+ residents
- Additional notification requirements

**HIPAA requirement:** 60 days
**New Jersey requirement:** 60 days (same as HIPAA)

**You must comply with both requirements.**
